# Culture Seeder Language Pack Reference

## Purpose

This document is the maintainer reference for the real-world language, accent, script and mutual-intelligibility content owned by `CultureSeeder`. The companion ethnicity and broader-culture matrix is documented in the [Culture Seeder Heritage Pack Reference](./Culture_Seeder_Heritage_Pack_Reference.md).

The stock goal is coverage rather than a claim that every historical speech community fits a modern language boundary. Every real-world naming culture installed by a culture pack must have at least one defensible language in the same standalone pack. Broad or composite naming cultures may map to several languages when the culture represents a court, contact zone, literary tradition or multilingual polity.

## Seeder Behaviour

The language question is offered for:

- `Earth-Modern`
- `Earth-Antiquity`
- `Earth-DarkAgesAndMedieval`
- `Earth-RenaissanceEurope`
- `Earth-RenaissanceWorldExpansion`
- `Middle-Earth`

`Earth-DarkAgesAndMedieval` and `Earth-RenaissanceWorldExpansion` are complete standalone language packs. They do not require the Renaissance-Europe or Modern pack to have been run first.

The two historical catalogues are data-driven:

- Dark Ages and Medieval: 25 naming-culture mappings, 36 language definitions, 15 scripts and 23 conservative mutual-intelligibility pairs.
- Renaissance World Expansion: 25 naming-culture mappings, 52 language definitions, 19 scripts and 17 conservative mutual-intelligibility pairs.

Every defined language receives:

- the standard `Foreign` learner accent;
- at least one historically grounded native accent or reading tradition;
- a `crude` learner accent;
- an upserted language trait and skill-cap expression.

## Historical Abstraction Policy

Use a separate language when one or more of these are true:

- ordinary speakers should not receive comfortable automatic comprehension;
- the chronological difference is important to play, such as Old English versus Middle English;
- a modern umbrella would project a later standard backwards, such as Shona over Karanga, Korekore and Zezuru;
- scholarship treats the varieties as distinct languages, such as the major Tibetic and Amazigh languages;
- the learned written register functions as a separate gameplay competency, such as Literary or Classical Chinese.

Use accents within one language when the varieties are regionally meaningful but remain suitable for one language skill. Examples include Old English's West Saxon, Mercian, Northumbrian and Kentish varieties, and one shared Medieval Gaelic language with Irish and Scottish accents.

Mutual intelligibility is deliberately conservative. A missing link means that resemblance, shared loanwords or a common script is not enough for free spoken comprehension. Shared writing systems never imply spoken mutual intelligibility by themselves.

Some languages at the selected anchor were primarily oral. The seeder does not invent an indigenous script for Kikongo, Karanga, Korekore, Zezuru or comparable traditions merely to make every language-script graph symmetrical.

Important design decisions:

- Medieval Gaelic is one language with Irish and Scottish accents. The modern Irish/Scottish language split is not projected back over the c. 1100-1250 shared learned tradition.
- Literary Chinese is a separate learned language with Song, Goryeo and Japanese Kanbun reading traditions. It shares Han characters with regional languages but has no spoken intelligibility links to Chinese, Korean or Japanese.
- Carolingian coverage uses attested Old High German and Old Low Franconian rather than inventing a single uniform Frankish standard.
- The Imperial German and Christian Iberian rows remain multilingual instead of projecting later High German or Castilian dominance backward over northern Germany and the other medieval Iberian Romance communities.
- Script coverage includes Latin, Futhorc, Younger Futhark, Greek, Cyrillic, Glagolitic, Arabic, Old Hungarian, Mongolian, Devanagari, Tamil, Grantha, Chinese, Idu and Japanese writing.

Important design decisions:

- Ming Guanhua is periodised separately from modern Standard Mandarin. Wu, Yue and the Southern, Eastern and Northern Min branches remain separate languages.
- Southern, Eastern and Northern Min are separate language skills rather than accents of one fictive spoken Min standard. Somali and Maay are likewise separate.
- Middle Korean receives both Chinese characters and the newly promulgated Korean alphabet appropriate to the 1443-1446 anchor.
- Kurmanji and Gorani are distinct languages. Central, Khams and Amdo Tibetan are also separate spoken languages alongside Classical Tibetan.
- Karanga, Korekore and Zezuru replace anachronistic use of a later unified Shona standard.
- Tashelhit, Central Atlas Tamazight, Tarifit, Kabyle and Nafusi replace a single undifferentiated new `Berber` language. The existing `Mahgrebi Arabic` spelling is retained as a stable compatibility key.
- Script coverage includes Armenian, Georgian secular and ecclesiastical hands, Arabic, Coptic, Nagari, Bengali, Kannada, Telugu, Tamil, Chinese, Korean, Japanese, Tibetan, Chu Nom, Thai, Kawi, Jawi and Ethiopic writing.

## Repeatability and Mixed-Pack Contract

The following keys are upsert contracts:

- language: case-insensitive language name;
- language trait: case-insensitive trait name;
- accent: language id plus case-insensitive accent name;
- mutual intelligibility: directed listener-language and heard-language pair;
- script and script knowledge: case-insensitive script name;
- script-language membership: script id plus language id.

Shared scripts use additive union semantics. Seeding another pack must not delete prior script-language memberships or builder-added memberships. The script acquisition FutureProg is rebuilt from the union of the requested and existing language memberships, so a Latin, Greek, Arabic or Chinese script remains selectable for every installed language that uses it regardless of pack order.

When `ChargenFreeKnowledges` exists, the Culture seeder also reconciles a marked stock section that grants each CultureSeeder-owned script knowledge for free when the chargen has `Literacy` and at least one selected skill accepted by that script's acquisition prog. Because the free-knowledge rule invokes the acquisition prog directly, shared-script language unions flow into chargen without another hard-coded language list. If Character Creation is seeded later, `ChargenSeeder` performs the same reconciliation from the installed script records.

Only script records whose knowledge uses the canonical `CanPick<ScriptName>ScriptKnowledge` prog are included. Builder text outside the managed Culture marker pair is preserved. Unsafe custom prog bodies are left unchanged with a warning rather than being rewritten.

Rerunning a pack repairs stock descriptions, difficulty values, accent fields, language traits, script metadata and mutual-intelligibility difficulty without duplicating stable rows.

## Verification Contract

`CultureSeederLanguageCoverageTests` verifies:

- both formerly empty historical packs offer the language question;
- every one of their 25 generated naming cultures has a coverage row;
- every coverage, script and intelligibility reference resolves to a language defined by that standalone pack;
- every defined language has a native accent;
- targeted older-pack gap closures stay present;
- shared script memberships use additive upserts;
- the corrected Modern Korean, Chinese, Hebrew, Arabic and Indic script definitions target their actual languages.
