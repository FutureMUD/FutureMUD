# FutureMUD Early Modern Culture Manifest Reference

## Purpose

This manifest partitions Early Modern item, skin, outfit, craft, shop, institution, and shared-baseline admission work by historically anchored culture families. It is not a claim that each family is culturally uniform.

## Prototype and skin rule

Create a culture-family prototype only when silhouette, material behaviour, component, institution, production technology, or contact/export form changes. Local names, script, colours, textile or porcelain motifs, heraldry, dynasty/company/regimental marks, inscriptions, and status normally belong in skins.

## Required manifest fields

Each entry records culture cluster, narrower inspiration, date anchor, social/institutional context, stable reference or skin target, shared-baseline admission, prototype justification, required dependencies, and sensitivity note where relevant.

## Tag status and acceptance

`Era / Early Modern Era` is live. Culture-cluster tags are not yet maintained stock; their exact hierarchy must be designed and seeded idempotently before item rows depend on it.

The completed manifest must cover every cluster above, distinguish local continuity from imported/contact goods, and avoid presenting colonial or coercive systems as culturally neutral universals.
