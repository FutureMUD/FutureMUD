# FutureMUD Renaissance Culture Manifest Reference

## Purpose

This manifest partitions Renaissance item, skin, outfit, craft, shop, institution, and shared-stock admission work by historically anchored culture families for approximately 1400-1600 CE.

## Prototype and skin rule

Create a culture-family prototype only where silhouette, material behaviour, component, institution, production technology, or contact/export form changes. Local names, script, motifs, heraldry, guild/dynastic marks, inscriptions, glaze/lacquer patterns, and status usually belong in skins.

## Manifest fields and tag status

Record culture cluster, narrower inspiration, date anchor, social/institutional context, stable reference or skin target, shared-stock admission, prototype justification, dependencies, and contact/sensitivity notes.

`Era / Renaissance Era` is live. Culture-cluster tags are not maintained stock; design and seed their exact hierarchy idempotently before branch items depend on them.

## Acceptance criteria

Every cluster above has a deliberate coverage decision, imported/contact goods are distinguished from local continuity, and early colonial systems are not presented as neutral or universal.
