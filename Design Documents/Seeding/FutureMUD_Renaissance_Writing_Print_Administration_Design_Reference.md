# FutureMUD Renaissance Writing, Print, and Administration Design Reference

## Scope and reuse

This branch extends the 39 shared `preindustrial_writing_*` aliases and minimum print set into Renaissance-specific manuscript, print, map, engraving, book-trade, civic, legal, and administrative forms.

Reuse the shared hand press, type case, composing stick, chase, inking balls, drying rack, broadside, pamphlet, almanac, blank form, and printed map sheet.

## Planned slices

- warrants, decrees, port/customs papers, notarial instruments, sketchbooks, commonplace books, pilot books, portolan charts, indulgence-like forms, prayer books, and printed music;
- type sorts, punches/matrices, type moulds, locking furniture, tympan/frisket assemblies, ink tables, and missing production stock;
- woodblocks, copper plates, burins, etching needles, engraving tools, daubers, carving knives, and printed images;
- East Asian brush, ink, paper, scroll, stitched-book, and woodblock systems where form/workflow differs from European movable type;
- seal matrices, sealing media, archive furniture, bookshop display, courier goods, and institution-specific record storage.

## Dependency contract

Paper, parchment, oak, leather, linen, brass, copper, lead, type metal, and printing ink are live. Papermaking, Bookbinding, Scribing, Calligraphy, Woodblock Printing, Movable Type Printing/Printer, and Engraving/Engraver skills exist. Administration and Law remain optional professional skills.

Readable/writeable/fixed-content component decisions must precede printed-content claims.

## Boundary and acceptance

Regular newspapers/journals, mature postal/financial paper systems, modern envelopes, staples, paperclips, binders, fountain pens, and modern office goods belong later. Every era row must differ from shared stock by format, component, institution, production technology, or gameplay role.
